\section{Conclusion}
\label{Conc}

We introduced a method that synthesizes a quantum circuit from a logic network, by using an ESOP expression as
a base, and then synthesizing a logic network with the ESOP's cubes as outputs. It then uses Boolean
Fourier Transform to precalculate a three-qubit or fewer phase oracle library that can be used to drive the
phase. We find that the T-count and T-depth produced by such a synthesis method improve on T-count and T-depth
compared to Qiskit's \texttt{PhaseOracle} and Boolean function based mappings such as {\it Caterpillar}. Results
have shown that further consideration should be given to specialized methods such as these in order to adapt
methods that have already been developed for quantum oracles in order to reduce T-count and T-depth. Some 
future work may involve incorporating quantum knowledge into the area reduction step of the logic network.
